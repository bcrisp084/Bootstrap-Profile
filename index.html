About Me

My name is Brian Crisp and i am a student at the Johns Hopkins University School of Engineering to become a full stack web developer. I don't have very much experience or knowledge of web page design but that is steadily changing. So far the tools that i have learned in this course have been tremendous in helping me understand and implement them into not only the class projects but also some things that i have always wanted to attempt on my own. I am currently trying to put together a web page for my wife's newly opened dog grooming shop. The Bootstrap framework that was used to create this portfolio has been such a big help in assisting in the design process. Having the ability to copy and paste frame work pieces that someone has created for the benefit of others has started to open my eyes to what the capabilities are and how much faster you can get things done when you don't always have to code from scratch. It is amazing when you look back at these very large files of code and see what they interpret on the user's screen. I never realized how much was involved in the coding process itself and how many different languages there are that don't just work independently from one another but in unison.I currently work as a technician at Mercedes Benz and attend the program in my spare time. I wasn't sure if web development was something that i would just try to dabble with as a side hobby or if it would turn into something that could be my new career moving forward but the more i work on things in and out of the class the more aparent it is becoming to me that this maybe the career i have been looking for my whole life.
